# 1. PREAMBLE AND SCOPE OF APPLICATION

## 1.1 Purpose and Intent

This Comprehensive Flight Rebooking, Modification, and Change Policy (hereinafter referred to as "the Policy" or "this
Policy") establishes the complete framework governing all requests by passengers to modify, alter, amend, reschedule,
reroute, or otherwise change any aspect of a confirmed reservation for air transportation services operated by Embabel
Air Corporation, its wholly-owned subsidiaries, and affiliated carriers operating under the Embabel Air brand pursuant
to franchise agreements executed in accordance with the Master Franchise Agreement dated March 15, 2019, as amended from
time to time.

## 1.2 Scope of Application

### 1.2.1 Geographic Applicability

This Policy shall apply to:

(a) All flights departing from any airport within the Continental United States of America, including the District of
Columbia but excluding, for purposes of Sections 7 through 12 only, the unincorporated territories of Guam, American
Samoa, the United States Virgin Islands, and the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands, which territories shall
be governed by the provisions of Appendix T-1;

(b) All flights arriving at any airport within the Continental United States of America from international origins,
subject to the superseding provisions of applicable bilateral air service agreements and the limitations set forth in
Section 20;

(c) All international flights operated by Embabel Air under its own operating certificate (DOT Certificate Number
2019-7-34), excluding codeshare flights operated by partner carriers except where the Marketing Carrier Exception
applies pursuant to Section 20.4.7;

(d) Domestic flights within the European Union where Embabel Air holds valid operating authority, subject to the
mandatory passenger rights provisions of Regulation (EC) No 261/2004, which shall take precedence over conflicting
provisions of this Policy to the extent required by law;

(e) Transborder services between the United States and Canada, subject to the provisions of the U.S.-Canada Air
Transport Agreement and applicable Canadian Transportation Agency regulations.

### 1.2.2 Passenger Eligibility

This Policy applies to all categories of passengers, including but not limited to:

(a) Revenue passengers holding tickets purchased directly from Embabel Air through any authorized sales channel;

(b) Award ticket holders who have redeemed Embabel Altitude Points for air transportation;

(c) Passengers traveling on tickets purchased through authorized third-party intermediaries, subject to the Additional
Processing Fee provisions of Section 7.8;

(d) Passengers traveling under corporate discount agreements, subject to the modifications set forth in Section 22;

(e) Non-revenue passengers, including employees traveling on staff benefits and persons traveling on companion passes,
who shall be subject to the Non-Revenue Passenger Supplement attached hereto as Appendix N-1;

(f) Unaccompanied minors, subject to the additional requirements of Section 14.9 and the Unaccompanied Minor Change
Protocol.

### 1.2.3 Excluded Transactions

Notwithstanding the foregoing, this Policy shall NOT apply to:

(a) Cancellations without rebooking, which are governed exclusively by the Embabel Air Cancellation and Refund Policy (
Document Reference: EMB-POL-2024-CXL-001);

(b) Involuntary changes initiated by Embabel Air due to schedule changes, aircraft substitutions, or operational
requirements, which are governed by the Involuntary Accommodation Policy (Document Reference: EMB-POL-2024-IVR-001);

(c) Changes necessitated by circumstances meeting the definition of Force Majeure Events as set forth in Section 2.47;

(d) Modifications to ancillary services only, such as seat selection changes, baggage additions, or meal preference
updates, which do not affect the core itinerary elements defined in Section 2.31;

(e) Name corrections of fewer than three (3) characters that do not change the identity of the traveler, which are
processed under the Minor Correction Protocol.

## 1.3 Effective Date and Transition Provisions

### 1.3.1 Commencement

This Policy shall become effective at 00:01 Eastern Standard Time on January 1, 2024 (the "Effective Date") and shall
apply to:

(a) All change requests submitted on or after the Effective Date, regardless of the original booking date;

(b) All change requests in progress as of the Effective Date that have not been fully processed and confirmed, subject
to the Grandfather Provisions of Section 1.3.2.

### 1.3.2 Grandfather Provisions

Where a change request was submitted prior to the Effective Date but remains pending as of such date:

(a) If the applicable change fee under the Prior Policy (Document Reference: EMB-POL-2023-RBK-001-Rev.14) would result
in a lower total charge to the passenger, the passenger may elect to have the Prior Policy applied, provided such
election is communicated in writing to the Rebooking Processing Center within seven (7) Business Days of the Effective
Date;

(b) If no election is made within the prescribed period, this Policy shall apply automatically;

(c) In no event shall a passenger be entitled to selective application of provisions from both the Prior Policy and this
Policy (the "Cherry-Picking Prohibition").

### 1.3.3 Survival

The following provisions of the Prior Policy shall survive the Effective Date and continue in full force and effect with
respect to transactions initiated prior thereto:

(a) Section 16 (Appeals in Progress);
(b) Section 24 (Credit Vouchers Issued Prior to Effective Date);
(c) Appendix R-2 (Legacy Fare Class Mapping Table).

## 1.4 Relationship to Other Policies

### 1.4.1 Hierarchy of Documents

In the event of any conflict or inconsistency between this Policy and other Embabel Air policy documents, the following
hierarchy shall apply, with documents listed first taking precedence:

(a) Embabel Air Conditions of Carriage (Document Reference: EMB-COC-2024-001);
(b) This Policy;
(c) Embabel Altitude Loyalty Program Terms and Conditions;
(d) Fare Rules applicable to the specific ticket;
(e) General Sales Agent Agreements (where applicable);
(f) Published fare filing tariffs.

### 1.4.2 Regulatory Override

Notwithstanding Section 1.4.1, where any provision of this Policy conflicts with applicable law, regulation, or binding
government order, such law, regulation, or order shall prevail, and this Policy shall be deemed modified to the minimum
extent necessary to achieve compliance, with all other provisions remaining in full force and effect (the "Severability
Principle").

# 3. EMBABEL ALTITUDE LOYALTY PROGRAM TIER STRUCTURE

## 3.1 Overview of Tier Hierarchy

The Embabel Altitude Rewards Program maintains a four-tier structure that determines member benefits, including
rebooking fee modifications. The tiers, in ascending order of privilege, are:

(a) **No Status (Standard Membership)**: Base level enrollment with no elite benefits
(b) **Bronze Status**: First tier of elite status
(c) **Silver Status**: Second tier of elite status  
(d) **Gold Status**: Highest tier of elite status

### 3.1.1 Status Recognition

Tier status is indicated in the Member's Altitude account and on their digital membership card. Status is verified at
the time of Change Request submission and must be active as of the Transaction Date to receive applicable fee
modifications.

### 3.1.2 Status Determination Timing

For purposes of applying Tier-Based Fee Modifications under Section 8, the Member's status shall be determined as of:

(a) The Transaction Date of the Change Request; OR

(b) The Original Booking Date, if the Member held a higher status at that time (the "Status Protection Rule"), provided
that such higher status was achieved through legitimate qualification activities and not subsequently revoked for cause.

## 3.2 Bronze Status Qualification

### 3.2.1 Qualification Thresholds

A Member shall qualify for Bronze Status upon satisfying ANY ONE of the following qualification pathways during a single
Qualification Year:

**Pathway A (Flight-Based Qualification):**

- Minimum 25,000 Elite Qualifying Miles; AND
- Minimum $2,500 Elite Qualifying Dollars

**Pathway B (Segment-Based Qualification):**

- Minimum 30 Elite Qualifying Segments; AND
- Minimum $2,500 Elite Qualifying Dollars

**Pathway C (Spend-Based Qualification):**

- Minimum $5,000 Elite Qualifying Dollars (with no EQM or EQS minimum)

**Pathway D (Credit Card Qualification):**

- Minimum $25,000 annual spend on the Embabel Altitude Elite Credit Card; AND
- Minimum 10 Elite Qualifying Segments

### 3.2.2 Bronze Status Benefits Relevant to Rebooking

Members holding Bronze Status shall receive the following benefits applicable to this Policy:

(a) 15% reduction in Base Change Fees (the "Bronze Tier Multiplier");
(b) Extended Change Windows as specified in Section 13.2.2;
(c) Priority processing for Change Requests (five Business Day standard);
(d) Access to Bronze-tier customer service line for change assistance;
(e) Eligibility for one complimentary Same-Day Flight Change per calendar year.

## 3.3 Silver Status Qualification

### 3.3.1 Qualification Thresholds

A Member shall qualify for Silver Status upon satisfying ANY ONE of the following qualification pathways during a single
Qualification Year:

**Pathway A (Flight-Based Qualification):**

- Minimum 50,000 Elite Qualifying Miles; AND
- Minimum $6,000 Elite Qualifying Dollars

**Pathway B (Segment-Based Qualification):**

- Minimum 60 Elite Qualifying Segments; AND
- Minimum $6,000 Elite Qualifying Dollars

**Pathway C (Spend-Based Qualification):**

- Minimum $12,000 Elite Qualifying Dollars (with no EQM or EQS minimum)

**Pathway D (Credit Card Qualification):**

- Minimum $50,000 annual spend on the Embabel Altitude Elite Credit Card; AND
- Minimum 25 Elite Qualifying Segments; AND
- Minimum $3,000 Elite Qualifying Dollars from flights

### 3.3.2 Silver Status Benefits Relevant to Rebooking

Members holding Silver Status shall receive the following benefits applicable to this Policy:

(a) 30% reduction in Base Change Fees (the "Silver Tier Multiplier");
(b) Further Extended Change Windows as specified in Section 13.2.3;
(c) Expedited processing for Change Requests (three Business Day standard);
(d) Access to dedicated Silver Elite service line for change assistance;
(e) Eligibility for two complimentary Same-Day Flight Changes per calendar year;
(f) Waiver of Late Change Surcharges for up to two changes per calendar year;
(g) Fee-free changes for flights booked more than 60 days in advance.

## 3.4 Gold Status Qualification

### 3.4.1 Qualification Thresholds

A Member shall qualify for Gold Status upon satisfying ANY ONE of the following qualification pathways during a single
Qualification Year:

**Pathway A (Flight-Based Qualification):**

- Minimum 75,000 Elite Qualifying Miles; AND
- Minimum $12,000 Elite Qualifying Dollars

**Pathway B (Segment-Based Qualification):**

- Minimum 100 Elite Qualifying Segments; AND
- Minimum $12,000 Elite Qualifying Dollars

**Pathway C (Spend-Based Qualification):**

- Minimum $25,000 Elite Qualifying Dollars (with no EQM or EQS minimum)

**Pathway D (Million Miler Lifetime Qualification):**

- Lifetime accumulation of 1,000,000 Elite Qualifying Miles with Embabel Air; AND
- Minimum $3,000 Elite Qualifying Dollars in the current year (to remain Active Gold status)

**Pathway E (Credit Card + Flying Qualification):**

- Minimum $100,000 annual spend on the Embabel Altitude Elite Credit Card; AND
- Minimum 50 Elite Qualifying Segments; AND
- Minimum $6,000 Elite Qualifying Dollars from flights

### 3.4.2 Gold Status Benefits Relevant to Rebooking

Members holding Gold Status shall receive the following comprehensive benefits applicable to this Policy:

(a) **50% reduction** in Base Change Fees (the "Gold Tier Multiplier");

(b) **Maximum Extended Change Windows** as specified in Section 13.2.4, including the ability to make changes up to
two (2) hours prior to departure for domestic flights;

(c) **Priority expedited processing** for Change Requests (same Business Day standard for requests submitted by 14:00
local time);

(d) **Access to the Gold Concierge Desk**, a dedicated 24/7 service line staffed by senior agents with enhanced
authorization to process complex changes and approve exceptions;

(e) **Unlimited complimentary Same-Day Flight Changes** for confirmed space on earlier or later flights in the same
booking class;

(f) **Complete waiver of Late Change Surcharges** for all changes regardless of timing;

(g) **Fee-free changes** for any flight booked more than 21 days in advance, provided the change is made at least 7 days
before departure;

(h) **Companion Change Protection**: When a Gold Member changes a booking, up to one Traveling Companion on the same PNR
may receive identical fee treatment regardless of the companion's personal tier status;

(i) **Upgrade Change Flexibility**: When changing to a flight with different upgrade availability, Gold Members receive
priority consideration for upgrade reinstatement;

(j) **Residual Value Protection**: Any Residual Value from downgrading to a less expensive fare is retained as Travel
Credit with no forfeiture, valid for 24 months;

(k) **Emergency Override Authority**: Gold Members may invoke a one-time annual Emergency Override to waive all fees for
any change, subject to completion of post-travel documentation;

(l) **Redeposit Fee Waiver**: Complete waiver of Redeposit Fees for Award Ticket changes.

## 3.5 Status Maintenance Requirements

### 3.5.1 Annual Requalification

Elite status must be re-earned each Qualification Year (January 1 through December 31) by meeting the applicable
qualification thresholds. Status earned in one Qualification Year is valid for the entirety of the following Status
Year (January 1 through December 31).

### 3.5.2 Soft Landing Provisions

Members who fail to requalify for their current tier status but achieve at least 50% of the qualification requirements
shall be granted a one-tier reduction rather than full status loss:

(a) Gold Members achieving 50-99% of Gold requirements receive Silver Status;
(b) Silver Members achieving 50-99% of Silver requirements receive Bronze Status;
(c) Bronze Members achieving 50-99% of Bronze requirements receive a 90-day status extension.

### 3.5.3 Status Challenge Program

New Members or Members returning after a gap of two or more years may be eligible for the Status Challenge Program,
allowing accelerated qualification over a 90-day period with reduced thresholds. Status Challenge participants receive
provisional benefits including Change Fee modifications at the challenged tier level during the challenge period.

## 3.6 Status Holds and Suspensions

### 3.6.1 Administrative Status Hold

Embabel Air may place a Member's status on administrative hold pending investigation of:

(a) Suspected fraudulent qualification activity;
(b) Abuse of Change Fee waivers or complimentary change benefits;
(c) Chargebacks or payment disputes exceeding $500;
(d) Violation of the Altitude Program Terms and Conditions.

### 3.6.2 Effect of Status Hold

During a status hold period:

(a) Tier-Based Fee Modifications shall not be applied to Change Requests;
(b) Complimentary change benefits shall be suspended;
(c) The Member shall be treated as a No Status Member for all purposes of this Policy;
(d) Upon resolution favorable to the Member, all withheld benefits shall be restored retroactively.

# 4. ANNUAL QUALIFYING SPEND CATEGORIES AND CALCULATION METHODOLOGY

## 4.1 Purpose and Application

Annual Qualifying Spend (AQS) serves as an independent factor in determining Change Fee modifications, separate from and
in addition to tier status benefits. This Section establishes the methodology for calculating AQS and the resulting
Spend-Based Adjustments applicable under Section 9.

## 4.2 AQS Threshold Categories

For purposes of Spend-Based Adjustments to Change Fees, Members are categorized according to the following AQS
thresholds:

| AQS Category | Annual Qualifying Spend Range | Category Code |
|--------------|-------------------------------|---------------|
| Category 1   | $0 - $999                     | AQS-1         |
| Category 2   | $1,000 - $2,499               | AQS-2         |
| Category 3   | $2,500 - $4,999               | AQS-3         |
| Category 4   | $5,000 - $9,999               | AQS-4         |
| Category 5   | $10,000 - $19,999             | AQS-5         |
| Category 6   | $20,000 - $34,999             | AQS-6         |
| Category 7   | $35,000 - $49,999             | AQS-7         |
| Category 8   | $50,000 - $74,999             | AQS-8         |
| Category 9   | $75,000 - $99,999             | AQS-9         |
| Category 10  | $100,000+                     | AQS-10        |

## 4.3 Calculation Period Definition

### 4.3.1 Standard Calculation Period

The AQS Calculation Period consists of the twelve (12) full calendar months immediately preceding the first day of the
month in which the Change Request is submitted.

**Example:** For a Change Request submitted on March 15, 2024, the Calculation Period runs from March 1, 2023 through
February 29, 2024.

### 4.3.2 Alternative Calculation Methods

At the Member's election, made by notation in the Change Request or via the Altitude Account Portal, the following
alternative calculation periods may be used:

(a) **Rolling 365-Day Method**: The 365-day period ending on the day prior to the Change Request submission;

(b) **Calendar Year Method**: The most recently completed calendar year, if the Change Request is submitted in the first
quarter and this method yields a higher AQS;

(c) **Blended Method**: A weighted average of the prior calendar year (weighted 60%) and the current partial year
annualized (weighted 40%), available only for Change Requests submitted after July 1.

### 4.3.3 Effective AQS Date

Where the Standard Calculation Period and an Alternative Calculation Method would yield different AQS Categories, the
higher category shall apply (the "AQS Optimization Rule").

## 4.4 Qualifying Purchases: Inclusions

The following purchases count toward AQS calculation at their full value:

### 4.4.1 Flight-Related Purchases

(a) Base fares for flights operated by Embabel Air;
(b) Carrier-imposed surcharges on Embabel Air flights;
(c) Cabin upgrade fees paid at time of booking;
(d) Paid upgrade fees for day-of-departure upgrades;
(e) Premium seat selection fees;
(f) Extra legroom seating fees.

### 4.4.2 Ancillary Service Purchases

(a) Checked baggage fees (first through tenth bag);
(b) Overweight and oversized baggage fees;
(c) Priority boarding purchases;
(d) Inflight Wi-Fi purchases (billed directly to Embabel Air);
(e) Inflight duty-free purchases exceeding $50;
(f) Unaccompanied minor service fees;
(g) Pet-in-cabin fees;
(h) Same-Day Flight Change fees (counted at 150% of fee paid).

### 4.4.3 Partner Purchases (Counted at 50% Value)

(a) Base fares for flights operated by Partner Carriers when booked through Embabel Air;
(b) Qualifying hotel stays booked through Embabel Travel;
(c) Qualifying car rentals booked through Embabel Travel;
(d) Embabel Vacations package purchases (flight component only).

### 4.4.4 Credit Card and Financial Products

(a) Embabel Altitude Credit Card annual fee (counted at 200% of fee amount);
(b) Points purchases at standard rate (counted at 50% of purchase price);
(c) Gift card purchases when subsequently redeemed for flight purchases.

## 4.5 Qualifying Purchases: Exclusions

The following purchases are EXCLUDED from AQS calculation:

### 4.5.1 Government-Imposed Charges

(a) Passenger facility charges (PFCs);
(b) Transportation Security Administration (TSA) fees;
(c) U.S. Federal Excise Tax;
(d) U.S. Immigration fees;
(e) U.S. Customs fees;
(f) International departure taxes;
(g) Foreign value-added taxes;
(h) Any other government-mandated fees or taxes.

### 4.5.2 Award and Promotional Travel

(a) Award tickets redeemed using Altitude Points (the points redemption value is excluded; only co-pays or taxes paid in
cash are counted);
(b) Complimentary tickets issued for service recovery;
(c) Tickets purchased using 100% Travel Credit or Credit Vouchers;
(d) System-wide upgrade awards;
(e) Companion tickets issued under promotional programs;
(f) Contest or sweepstakes prize tickets.

### 4.5.3 Refunded and Disputed Amounts

(a) Any fare or fee subsequently refunded to the original form of payment;
(b) Any charge subject to a pending chargeback dispute;
(c) Any charge successfully reversed through credit card dispute;
(d) Amounts credited back as Travel Credit (excluded at time of credit issuance).

### 4.5.4 Non-Qualifying Transactions

(a) Change Fees paid under this Policy (with the exception of Same-Day Change fees per Section 4.4.2(h));
(b) Cancellation fees;
(c) No-show fees;
(d) Name change fees;
(e) Redeposit fees for award tickets;
(f) Interest charges on Embabel Air credit products;
(g) Travel insurance premiums;
(h) Third-party lounge access purchases;
(i) Inflight food and beverage purchases under $50;
(j) Seat selection fees for seats not designated as "premium" or "extra legroom."

## 4.6 Special AQS Calculation Situations

### 4.6.1 New Members

Members enrolled in the Altitude Program for less than twelve (12) months shall have their AQS calculated as follows:

(a) Actual qualifying spend during the membership period, annualized by multiplying by (365 รท days of membership);

(b) Capped at the actual spend amount plus 25% (the "New Member Cap") to prevent excessive extrapolation from limited
data;

(c) Subject to a minimum floor equal to the actual spend amount regardless of membership duration.

### 4.6.2 Corporate Account Allocations

For Members whose travel is funded by a Corporate Account under Section 22:

(a) The Member receives AQS credit for 100% of the fare value for personally booked travel;

(b) The Member receives AQS credit for 75% of the fare value for travel booked by a corporate travel manager on their
behalf;

(c) The Member receives AQS credit for 50% of the fare value for travel booked through an Online Booking Tool with
corporate mandate applied.

### 4.6.3 Joint Account Holders

Where two or more Members hold a joint Altitude household account:

(a) AQS for each Member is calculated individually based on travel in that Member's name;

(b) For shared itineraries (same PNR), the AQS is split equally unless Members provide written allocation instructions;

(c) A combined household AQS may be used if both Members are on the same Change Request PNR and one Member has AQS-6 or
higher.

## 4.7 AQS Verification and Disputes

### 4.7.1 Verification Process

AQS category is verified automatically at the time of Change Request submission through the Altitude member database.
Members may request manual verification by contacting the Altitude Service Center.

### 4.7.2 AQS Disputes

Members who believe their AQS has been miscalculated may submit a dispute within thirty (30) days of a Change Request
by:

(a) Submitting the AQS Dispute Form (EMB-ALT-FORM-012);
(b) Providing documentation of claimed qualifying purchases;
(c) Identifying specific transactions believed to be missing or miscategorized.

### 4.7.3 Dispute Resolution Timeline

AQS disputes shall be resolved within fifteen (15) Business Days. If the dispute results in an AQS category increase
that would have resulted in a lower Change Fee, the difference shall be:

(a) Refunded to the original form of payment if the Member requests within ten (10) days of resolution; OR

(b) Applied as Travel Credit if no refund request is made.

# 6. CLASSIFICATION OF CHANGE REASONS

## 6.1 Overview and Purpose

The reason underlying a Change Request significantly affects the applicable fees and documentation requirements. This
Section establishes the comprehensive taxonomy of change reasons and their classification into fee categories.

## 6.2 Category A: Compassionate Circumstances (Maximum Fee Relief)

Category A encompasses circumstances invoking the highest level of fee relief, including complete waivers and expedited
processing.

### 6.2.1 Bereavement Events (Code: A-BRV)

**Definition:** The death of an Immediate Family Member occurring within the Bereavement Window Period.

**Eligible Relationships:**

- Spouse or domestic partner
- Parent or step-parent
- Child or step-child (including adopted and foster children)
- Sibling (full, half, or step)
- Grandparent
- Grandchild
- Parent-in-law
- Guardian or ward (with legal documentation)

**Fee Treatment:**

- Complete waiver of Base Change Fee
- Complete waiver of Processing Fee
- Complete waiver of Late Change Surcharge
- Fare Difference capped at 50% of calculated amount

**Documentation Required:** See Section 14.3.1

### 6.2.2 Serious Medical Emergency - Self (Code: A-MED-S)

**Definition:** A medical condition affecting the Affected Passenger that:
(i) Arose unexpectedly within 72 hours of scheduled departure; AND
(ii) Requires immediate medical treatment or hospitalization; AND
(iii) Renders air travel medically inadvisable or impossible.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Complete waiver of Base Change Fee
- Complete waiver of Processing Fee
- Complete waiver of Late Change Surcharge
- Complete waiver of Fare Difference

**Documentation Required:** See Section 14.3.2

### 6.2.3 Serious Medical Emergency - Immediate Family (Code: A-MED-F)

**Definition:** A medical condition affecting an Immediate Family Member of the Affected Passenger that:
(i) Arose unexpectedly; AND
(ii) Requires the immediate presence of the Affected Passenger; AND
(iii) Is certified as a genuine emergency by a Licensed Medical Professional.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Complete waiver of Base Change Fee
- Complete waiver of Processing Fee
- Waiver of Late Change Surcharge (50%)
- Fare Difference capped at 75% of calculated amount

**Documentation Required:** See Section 14.3.3

### 6.2.4 Military Deployment (Code: A-MIL)

**Definition:** A mandatory change in duty assignment issued to:
(i) Active duty members of the U.S. Armed Forces; OR
(ii) Activated members of the Reserve or National Guard; OR
(iii) Civilians with official emergency deployment orders from a government agency.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Complete waiver of all Change Fees
- Complete waiver of Fare Difference for downgrade; Fare Difference capped at 25% for upgrade
- Expedited processing (same-day guarantee)
- Extension of ticket validity to 24 months from original travel date

**Documentation Required:** See Section 14.3.4

### 6.2.5 Jury Duty (Code: A-JUR)

**Definition:** A mandatory summons for jury service that conflicts with scheduled travel and cannot be postponed.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Complete waiver of Base Change Fee
- Processing Fee reduced to $15
- Late Change Surcharge waived
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** See Section 14.3.5

### 6.2.6 Natural Disaster Displacement (Code: A-DIS)

**Definition:** The Affected Passenger's primary residence is located in an area subject to:
(i) A mandatory evacuation order; OR
(ii) A federal disaster declaration (FEMA); OR
(iii) Destruction or uninhabitability certified by local authorities.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Complete waiver of Base Change Fee for one change
- 75% waiver for subsequent changes
- Processing Fee waived
- Late Change Surcharge waived
- Fare Difference capped at actual cost increase to carrier

**Documentation Required:** See Section 14.3.6

## 6.3 Category B: Significant Life Events (Substantial Fee Relief)

Category B encompasses major life events that, while not emergencies, warrant meaningful fee accommodation.

### 6.3.1 Hospitalization - Non-Emergency (Code: B-HOS)

**Definition:** Scheduled or unscheduled hospitalization of the Affected Passenger for more than 24 hours, occurring
within 14 days of travel.

**Fee Treatment:**

- 75% waiver of Base Change Fee
- Processing Fee waived
- Late Change Surcharge waived
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** Hospital admission/discharge records

### 6.3.2 Pregnancy Complications (Code: B-PRG)

**Definition:** Medical advice restricting air travel due to pregnancy-related conditions, for travel originally booked
prior to the complication arising.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Complete waiver of Base Change Fee
- Processing Fee waived
- Late Change Surcharge waived
- Fare Difference capped at 50%

**Documentation Required:** Physician certification

### 6.3.3 Job Loss or Layoff (Code: B-JOB)

**Definition:** Involuntary termination of employment occurring after the original booking date.

**Fee Treatment:**

- 50% waiver of Base Change Fee
- Processing Fee applies normally
- Late Change Surcharge reduced by 50%
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** Termination letter or severance documentation

### 6.3.4 Divorce or Separation (Code: B-DIV)

**Definition:** Legal filing for divorce or documented separation occurring after the original booking date, affecting
joint travel plans.

**Fee Treatment:**

- 50% waiver of Base Change Fee
- Processing Fee waived for one traveler
- Late Change Surcharge applies normally
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** Court filing receipt or attorney letter

### 6.3.5 Victim of Crime (Code: B-CRM)

**Definition:** The Affected Passenger was the victim of a crime requiring police report filing and affecting ability to
travel as scheduled.

**Fee Treatment:**

- 75% waiver of Base Change Fee
- Processing Fee waived
- Late Change Surcharge waived
- Fare Difference capped at 75%

**Documentation Required:** Police report number and incident summary

## 6.4 Category C: Disrupted Travel (Moderate Fee Relief)

Category C encompasses situations where travel plans are disrupted by circumstances largely outside the passenger's
control.

### 6.4.1 Visa Denial (Code: C-VIS)

**Definition:** Denial of a required visa or travel authorization after application following original booking.

**Exclusions:** Does not apply where:

- Passenger failed to apply with reasonable lead time (minimum 30 days);
- Denial was due to passenger's failure to provide required documentation;
- Denial was based on criminal history known at time of booking.

**Fee Treatment:**

- 50% waiver of Base Change Fee
- Processing Fee applies normally
- Late Change Surcharge waived
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** Official denial letter

### 6.4.2 Passport Issues (Code: C-PAS)

**Definition:** Valid passport unavailable for travel due to:

- Theft (with police report);
- Loss during transit (with carrier documentation);
- Damage rendering passport invalid;
- Delayed renewal by government processing.

**Fee Treatment:**

- 40% waiver of Base Change Fee
- Processing Fee reduced by 50%
- Late Change Surcharge applies normally
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** Varies by situation

### 6.4.3 Connecting Transportation Failure (Code: C-CTF)

**Definition:** The passenger's scheduled transportation to the airport (train, bus, connecting flight on another
carrier) was cancelled or significantly delayed (2+ hours) due to causes beyond passenger control.

**Fee Treatment:**

- 50% waiver of Base Change Fee (for rescheduling only, not refund)
- Processing Fee applies normally
- Late Change Surcharge waived if request made within 4 hours of original departure
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** Documentation of cancellation/delay from transportation provider

### 6.4.4 Weather at Origin (Non-Embabel Cause) (Code: C-WXO)

**Definition:** Severe weather at the passenger's origin location preventing safe travel to the airport, where Embabel
Air operations are not affected.

**Fee Treatment:**

- 35% waiver of Base Change Fee
- Processing Fee applies normally
- Late Change Surcharge waived
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** National Weather Service advisory or equivalent

### 6.4.5 Vehicle Breakdown En Route (Code: C-VBD)

**Definition:** Mechanical failure of passenger's vehicle while traveling to the airport, with documented service call.

**Fee Treatment:**

- 25% waiver of Base Change Fee
- Processing Fee applies normally
- Late Change Surcharge reduced by 50%
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** Towing service receipt or roadside assistance confirmation

## 6.5 Category D: Schedule Conflicts (Limited Fee Relief)

Category D encompasses changes driven by schedule conflicts arising after booking.

### 6.5.1 Business Meeting Conflict (Code: D-BUS)

**Definition:** A mandatory business meeting scheduled by employer after original booking, conflicting with travel
plans.

**Requirements:**

- Must be submitted by corporate travel manager or HR department;
- Must be on company letterhead;
- Must certify meeting was scheduled after booking date.

**Fee Treatment:**

- 20% waiver of Base Change Fee
- Processing Fee applies normally
- Late Change Surcharge applies normally
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** Employer letter with specific meeting details

### 6.5.2 School Schedule Change (Code: D-SCH)

**Definition:** Official change to school calendar (not individually rescheduled events) affecting travel during
school-year periods.

**Fee Treatment:**

- 15% waiver of Base Change Fee
- Processing Fee applies normally
- Late Change Surcharge applies normally
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** School notification of calendar change

### 6.5.3 Event Cancellation or Rescheduling (Code: D-EVT)

**Definition:** The event that was the purpose of travel (concert, conference, sporting event) is cancelled or
rescheduled by the organizer.

**Fee Treatment:**

- 30% waiver of Base Change Fee for cancellation
- 15% waiver for rescheduling
- Processing Fee applies normally
- Late Change Surcharge waived for event cancellation
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** Event organizer notification

### 6.5.4 Wedding Postponement (Code: D-WED)

**Definition:** Postponement of a wedding the passenger was scheduled to attend, where the passenger is either a member
of the wedding party or immediate family.

**Fee Treatment:**

- 25% waiver of Base Change Fee
- Processing Fee waived
- Late Change Surcharge waived
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** Communication from bride/groom confirming postponement

## 6.6 Category E: Personal Preference Changes (No Fee Relief)

Category E encompasses changes made at the passenger's discretion without external cause.

### 6.6.1 Change of Plans (Code: E-COP)

**Definition:** Passenger elects to change travel dates, times, or routing for personal preference reasons.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Full Base Change Fee applies
- Full Processing Fee applies
- Full Late Change Surcharge applies
- Full Fare Difference applies

**Documentation Required:** None (standard change request only)

### 6.6.2 Found Better Fare (Code: E-FBF)

**Definition:** Passenger identifies a lower fare and wishes to change to that fare.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Full Base Change Fee applies
- Full Processing Fee applies
- Full Late Change Surcharge applies
- Fare Difference credit may be retained as Travel Credit subject to Section 17.7

**Documentation Required:** None

### 6.6.3 Upgrade Request (Code: E-UPG)

**Definition:** Passenger wishes to change to a higher cabin class on the same or different flight.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Base Change Fee reduced by 50% (upgrade incentive)
- Processing Fee applies normally
- Late Change Surcharge applies normally
- Fare Difference applies (positive amount required)

**Documentation Required:** None

### 6.6.4 Downgrade Request (Code: E-DWN)

**Definition:** Passenger wishes to change to a lower cabin class to reduce cost.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Full Base Change Fee applies
- Processing Fee applies normally
- Late Change Surcharge applies normally
- Residual Value retained as Travel Credit per Section 23

**Documentation Required:** None

## 6.7 Category F: Passenger Fault (Penalty Surcharges Apply)

Category F encompasses situations where the change is necessitated by passenger action or inaction.

### 6.7.1 Missed Flight - No Prior Contact (Code: F-MSN)

**Definition:** Passenger failed to board a flight for which they had a confirmed reservation, without contacting
Embabel Air prior to departure.

**Effect:** Remaining segments on the itinerary are automatically cancelled unless:

- Passenger contacts Embabel Air within 2 hours of departure; AND
- Passenger pays the Missed Flight Recovery Fee; AND
- Space is available on an alternative flight.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Full Base Change Fee applies
- Missed Flight Recovery Surcharge: $150 domestic / $300 international
- Processing Fee applies normally (doubled)
- Late Change Surcharge: Maximum applicable rate
- Fare Difference applies at higher of current fare or originally calculated difference
- Peak Period Surcharge doubled if applicable

**Documentation Required:** None accepted; fees apply regardless of reason

### 6.7.2 Missed Flight - Prior Contact Made (Code: F-MSC)

**Definition:** Passenger contacted Embabel Air within 60 minutes prior to departure to advise they would miss the
flight.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Full Base Change Fee applies
- Missed Flight Recovery Surcharge: $75 domestic / $150 international
- Processing Fee applies normally
- Late Change Surcharge applies at standard rate
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** Reference number from prior contact

### 6.7.3 Documentation Failure (Code: F-DOC)

**Definition:** Passenger failed to present required travel documentation (passport, visa, health certificates) at
check-in, preventing travel.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Full Base Change Fee applies
- Documentation Failure Surcharge: $100
- Processing Fee applies normally
- Late Change Surcharge applies at standard rate
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** None

### 6.7.4 Denied Boarding - Passenger Conduct (Code: F-DBC)

**Definition:** Passenger was denied boarding due to conduct issues, intoxication, failure to comply with crew
instructions, or similar cause.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Full Base Change Fee applies
- Conduct-Based Denial Surcharge: $250
- Processing Fee: Tripled
- Late Change Surcharge: Maximum rate
- Fare Difference: Higher of current fare or 150% of originally calculated difference
- Blackout period: No Same-Day Flight Change for 24 hours

**Documentation Required:** Appeal only with incident report and explanation

### 6.7.5 Incorrect Booking Information (Code: F-IBI)

**Definition:** Passenger provided incorrect information at booking (wrong dates, airports, etc.) and now seeks
correction.

**Fee Treatment:**

- Base Change Fee applies at 75% if corrected within 24 hours of booking
- Full Base Change Fee if corrected after 24 hours
- Processing Fee applies normally
- Late Change Surcharge applies if within Late Change Window
- Fare Difference applies normally

**Documentation Required:** None

## 6.8 Reason Classification Disputes

### 6.8.1 Passenger's Right to Classification Appeal

If a passenger believes their change reason has been misclassified, they may appeal by:

(a) Submitting the Reason Classification Appeal Form (EMB-RBK-FORM-003);
(b) Providing documentation supporting the claimed classification;
(c) Paying a $25 Appeal Filing Fee (refundable if appeal successful).

### 6.8.2 Appeal Timeline

Appeals must be filed within thirty (30) days of the original Change Request completion.

### 6.8.3 Appeal Decision

Appeal decisions are rendered within ten (10) Business Days. Decisions are final and not subject to further appeal,
except that Gold Members may request Executive Review under Section 16.6.

### 8.4.1 Automatic Verification

When a Change Request is submitted through an authenticated channel (logged-in web session, mobile app, or phone

Status verification is required when:

- The booking was made without linking an Altitude account;
- The Member has recently achieved a new tier status;
- The reservation shows a different Member number than the one making the request.

Acceptable proof of status includes:

- Digital Altitude membership card;
- Physical membership card with valid year indicator;
- Screenshot of account page showing status and Member number;
- Recent Altitude account statement.

### 8.5.1 Gold Companion Protection

Gold Members may extend their tier benefits to ONE Traveling Companion on the same PNR, regardless of the companion's
personal tier status.

### 8.5.2 Companion Requirements

To qualify for Companion Protection:

- Both passengers must be on the same PNR;
- The Gold Member must be the one submitting the Change Request (or authorize the companion to do so);
- The change must affect both passengers' itineraries identically.

### 8.5.3 Companion Limitations

Companion Protection does not extend to:

- More than one companion per PNR;
- Passengers on separate PNRs (even if on the same flights);
- Complimentary change benefits (only fee discounts apply).

## 8.6 Tier Benefits Stacking Prohibition

Tier-Based Fee Modifications are calculated based on a single tier status. Where multiple Members with different
statuses are on the same PNR:

(a) Each Member's fees are calculated based on their own status; OR

(b) The highest-tier Member may invoke the Gold Companion Protection (if Gold); OR

(c) All Members may elect to be treated at the lowest common tier status for simplified processing.

# 12. COMBINED FACTOR CALCULATION METHODOLOGY

## 12.1 Calculation Sequence

The final Change Fee is calculated using the following sequence:

1. **Determine Base Change Fee** (Section 7)
    - Identify route category
    - Identify fare family
    - Identify timing window
    - Look up Base Change Fee

2. **Apply Tier Multiplier** (Section 8)
    - Verify Member tier status
    - Apply tier multiplier
    - Result: Tier-Adjusted Fee

3. **Apply AQS Discount** (Section 9)
    - Determine AQS category
    - Apply AQS discount percentage
    - Result: Spend-Adjusted Fee

4. **Apply Points Balance Discount** (Section 10)
    - Determine Points Balance tier
    - Apply points discount percentage
    - Check against Discount Cap
    - Result: Points-Adjusted Fee

5. **Apply Reason-Based Adjustment** (Section 11)
    - Classify change reason
    - Apply waiver or reduction percentage
    - Result: Reason-Adjusted Fee

6. **Add Processing Fee** (Section 7.5)
    - Determine booking channel
    - Apply processing fee (subject to waivers)

7. **Add Late Change Surcharge** (Section 13, if applicable)
    - Determine if within Late Change Window
    - Calculate surcharge (subject to waivers)

8. **Add Peak Period Surcharge** (Section 19, if applicable)
    - Check travel date against Peak Calendar
    - Calculate surcharge (subject to waivers)

9. **Add Third-Party Booking Surcharge** (Section 7.8, if applicable)
    - Verify booking source
    - Apply surcharge

10. **Add Category F Surcharges** (Section 11.7, if applicable)
    - Apply fault-based surcharges
    - Apply multipliers

11. **Apply Floor and Ceiling Limits** (Section 7.7)
    - Verify minimum fee
    - Verify maximum fee cap

12. **Calculate Final Change Fee**

## 12.2 Master Calculation Formula

```
Final Change Fee = MIN(
  MAX(
    ((Base_Fee ร Tier_Mult ร AQS_Mult ร PB_Mult ร Reason_Mult) + Processing + Late_Surch + Peak_Surch + 3P_Surch + F_Surch),
    Minimum_Fee
  ),
  Maximum_Fee_Cap
)
```

Where:

- All multiplicative discounts are expressed as (1 - discount_percentage)
- Processing, surcharges may be $0 if waived
- Minimum_Fee from Section 7.7.1
- Maximum_Fee_Cap from Section 7.7.2

# 17. FARE DIFFERENCE CALCULATIONS

## 17.1 Fare Difference Principles

### 17.1.1 Definition

Fare Difference is the amount by which the fare for the new itinerary exceeds (Additional Collection) or falls below (
Residual Value) the Original Fare Value.

### 17.1.2 Fare Difference Is Separate

Fare Difference is always calculated and assessed separately from Change Fees. Discounts and waivers applying to Change
Fees do not automatically apply to Fare Difference unless specifically stated.

## 17.2 Original Fare Value Determination

### 17.2.1 Components Included

- Base fare as purchased
- Carrier-imposed surcharges (YQ/YR)
- Fuel surcharges
- Booking fees assessed by Embabel Air

### 17.2.2 Components Excluded

- Government taxes
- Passenger facility charges
- Security fees
- Immigration/customs fees
- Third-party booking fees
- Optional ancillary purchases

## 17.3 Additional Collection Limits

| Reason Category     | Additional Collection Limit           |
|---------------------|---------------------------------------|
| A-BRV               | 50% of calculated                     |
| A-MED-S             | Waived                                |
| A-MED-F             | 75% of calculated                     |
| A-MIL               | 25% for upgrade, waived for downgrade |
| A-JUR through A-DIS | Varies per Section 11                 |
| B through D         | No limit                              |
| E                   | No limit                              |
| F-MSN, F-DBC        | Higher of calculated or 150%          |
| F-MSC, F-DOC, F-IBI | No limit                              |

## 17.4 Residual Value Treatment by Tier

| Tier      | Residual Value Treatment                        |
|-----------|-------------------------------------------------|
| No Status | Non-refundable Travel Credit, 12-month validity |
| Bronze    | Non-refundable Travel Credit, 18-month validity |
| Silver    | Travel Credit 24-month OR refund minus $50      |
| Gold      | Refund (no fee) OR Travel Credit 36-month       |

## 17.5 Fare Difference Caps

| Situation                                | Maximum Fare Difference       |
|------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Same route, same class, date change only | 150% of Original Fare Value   |
| Route change within same region          | 200% of Original Fare Value   |
| Route change to different region         | No cap                        |
| Cabin upgrade                            | No cap (upgrade is voluntary) |
| Carrier-initiated schedule change        | No fare difference            |

## 17.6 Hardship Caps

| Reason                     | Maximum Additional Collection                     |
|----------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| Bereavement                | $200 or 50% of fare difference, whichever is less |
| Medical Emergency - Self   | $0                                                |
| Medical Emergency - Family | $300 or 75%, whichever is less                    |
| Natural Disaster           | Carrier's actual cost increase                    |
